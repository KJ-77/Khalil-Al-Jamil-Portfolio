## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary audience**: Recruiters, hiring managers, potential clients, and freelance leads
- **Context**: They're evaluating Khalil quickly — skimming for signals of competence, range, and professionalism. They may be comparing against other candidates' portfolios
- **Job to be done**: Quickly assess whether Khalil is a strong full-stack + DevOps candidate worth reaching out to

### Brand Personality
- **3 words**: Confident, distinctive, technical
- **Voice**: Professional but not corporate — shows personality without trying too hard
- **Emotional goals**: Visitor should feel "this person clearly knows what they're doing" while also thinking "this portfolio isn't like the other 50 I've seen today"

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Clean and confident as the foundation, with bold/modern touches that prevent it from feeling generic. Not maximalist — the boldness comes from intentional layout choices, strong hierarchy, and moments of visual surprise, not from visual noise
- **Theme**: Dark mode only
- **Anti-references**: Generic dev portfolios (same card grids, same layout, same feel). The typical "dark mode + gradient text + glass cards" AI-generated look
- **Key differentiator**: The full-stack + DevOps range is the story — the layout should make this breadth unmistakably clear within seconds

### Design Principles
1. **Hierarchy sells range**: The most important message is "I do frontend, backend, AND infrastructure." Layout and visual weight should make this impossible to miss
2. **Earned confidence**: Every element should feel intentional and polished — the quality of the portfolio IS proof of the quality of the work
3. **Stand out through structure, not decoration**: Differentiation comes from layout rhythm, spatial decisions, and typography — not from adding more effects or ornaments
4. **Respect the skim**: Recruiters scan in seconds. Critical info must land fast — then reward deeper reading for those who stay
